American

Find out about classic American dishes throughout time!

  • Early 1900's

    BBQ Brisket

    Jewish immigrants were the first to smoke brisket in the United States. By the early 1900s smoked brisket appeared on Jewish deli menus across Texas.

  • 1929 Lobster Role

    The lobster roll originated as a hot dish at a restaurant named Perry's in Milford, Connecticut, as early as 1929. Its popularity then spread up and down the Connecticut coast, but not far beyond it.

  • 1930

    Cheesestake

    In 1930, the cheesesteak was invented when Pat Olivieri, a hot dog vendor and namesake to Pat's King of Steaks, threw beef on his grill to make a sandwich.

  • 1964

    Buffalo Wings

    The concept of cooking wings in peppery hot sauce was born in 1964 at the Anchor Bar in Buffalo, New York, when co-owner Teressa Bellissimo cooked leftover wings in hot sauce as a late-night snack for her son and his friends.

Chinese

China has many different types of food!

Chinese food is one of the world's oldest and most diverse cuisines. It's known for its fresh ingredients, variety of flavors, and emphasis on texture, color, aroma, and flavor. Chinese meals typically include four main food groups: fruits, vegetables, grains, and meat, and are often low in fat and nutritious.

Japanese

See what Japan has to offer!

Japanese cuisine, or washoku, is based on fresh, local ingredients and seasonal offerings. It's closely tied to nature and the land and sea, and uses special cooking techniques and utensils to highlight natural flavors. The flavors are often mild, but can sometimes be strong or unfamiliar to Western tastes.

Korean

Korea has many delectable dishes!

Korean food is known for its complex flavors, which can be sweet, salty, spicy, and tangy. It often includes rice, noodles, vegetables, and meats, along with many side dishes called banchan. Fermented ingredients and condiments, such as jang, are also common.

Mongolian

Mongolian food is very popular in the United States.

Mongolian cuisine is known for being simple and varied, and is influenced by the country's nomadic lifestyle and climate. Traditional dishes often feature meat, dairy, and animal fats from a variety of animals, including cattle, mutton, horse, goat, and game.

Taiwanese

Tai food is one of my personal favorites!

Taiwanese food is known for its sweet and salty flavors, and is a mix of indigenous and outside influences. It's similar to Chinese cuisine, and meals often center around white rice and pork. Other common ingredients include chicken, seafood, tofu, soy, chestnuts, and water caltrop. Taiwanese cooking also uses a variety of seasonings, such as soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, fermented black beans, pickled radishes, peanuts, chili peppers, cilantro, and basil.

Tibetan

Tibetan is a lesser known food that is very tasty!

Tibetan food is known for being rustic and appetizing, and reflects the region's cold climate by featuring lots of soups, stews, and braises.
